Health Benefits of Eggs

High-Quality Protein

Eggs contain all 9 essential amino acids, making them a complete protein source for muscle building and repair.

Brain Health

Rich in choline, which is essential for brain development and function, memory, and nervous system health.

Eye Protection

Contains lutein and zeaxanthin, antioxidants that reduce the risk of cataracts and macular degeneration.

Heart Health

Moderate egg consumption can improve heart health by raising HDL (good) cholesterol levels.

Weight Management

High protein content helps you feel full longer, reducing overall calorie intake throughout the day.

Bone Strength

Good source of Vitamin D and phosphorus, essential for strong bones and teeth.

Eggs in Daily Life

Versatile Superfood

Eggs are one of the most versatile ingredients in cooking. Here's how to incorporate them into your daily routine:

  • Breakfast: Scrambled, boiled, or as omelets for sustained energy
  • Lunch: Added to salads, sandwiches, or fried rice
  • Dinner: In quiches, frittatas, or as protein topping
  • Baking: Essential for cakes, cookies, and pastries
  • Snacks: Hard-boiled eggs as portable, healthy snacks
